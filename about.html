System Overview & Model Documentation

How the prediction engine works

A machine learning forecasting system that estimates Bitcoin's next-day closing price using seven years of market history, engineered technical indicators, and a stacked ensemble of models validated the way real trading conditions actually unfold.

Validation & Evaluation Metrics

Time Series Validation

Market data is chronological, so random train/test splitting isn't appropriate — it can leak future information into training. This system uses TimeSeriesSplit Cross-Validation, which always trains on the past to predict the future, mirroring real market conditions.

Individual models capture different market behaviors on their own. Combining both through Ridge Regression improves overall robustness and reduces prediction instability.

Multiple models are trained across different validation periods, and their predictions are averaged into a final forecast — reducing variance and improving stability compared to any single model.

Data Preprocessing

Before training, the raw data goes through several cleaning and preparation steps:

  1. Remove missing values — incomplete rows are dropped
  2. Calculate technical indicators — RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, EMAs
  3. Generate lag features — prior-day and prior-week prices
  4. Exclude invalid rows — created during indicator calculations
  5. Align features to the past — ensures no future information leaks in
